THE LIFE AND DEATH OF DIGITAL SYMBOLS, AND WHEN TO KILL THEM
Abstract (The life and death of digital symbols, and when to kill them) by Caitlin Berner. For more information mail to: info@caitlinberner.com



Every year the Oxford dictionairy chooses a word of the year.
This year (2015) the word was actually an emoji. In my eyes this
represents the current world we live in perfectly. In stead of
using text as a medium for our information exchange we use more
and more imagery. This imagery exists mostly out of symbols and
icons. This language evolution is encouraged by the increased use
of computers in our daily lives. We communicate more via computers
and than ever before, and because of the rapid developments,
and fast-paced information flow we can allmost not keep up with,
the icons and symbols get a more important role in our daily lives.

When seeing the world mostly through a screen, knowing what is reality
and what is not, is a line that can get blurry. Especially when the digital
tries to recreate, or mimic reality it is difficult to create the divide
in our minds. (example: instagram people are not real, they take the right
pictures and use the right frame) What I want to talk about and what i see
happening a lot around me is that becuase of (the design of) icons and symbols
used on our computers and phones, the meaning of the object or situation that
symbol mimics loses it’s meaning or changes it. (example: a younger generation
only sees the on their screen, and thus when seeing a floppy disk
in real life they think it’s a 3d version of the icon on their screen. It is
a result of fast developments in technology, which make us replace the objects
we have very often. We forget to remember and history in a way is lost) It is
about how language and design come together and can alter meaning. This leads
me to my research question:

In what respect does the design of (digital)
symbols and icons effect the way we see the
(physical) world and do we have to re-think
the design accordingly?
